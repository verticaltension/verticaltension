## Proposed Posts (Round 1)

### 1) The Reckoning of Intelligence: What Happens When We Can Outsource Thinking?
- **Slug**: `the-reckoning-of-intelligence`
- **Description**: As AI becomes cheap, fast, and ubiquitous, the core question isn’t capability—it’s agency. This post frames the choice ahead: surrender decision-making to algorithmic proxies, or deliberately cultivate sovereign cognition.
- **Reading time**: 6 min read
- **Tags**: Agency, AI, Philosophy
- **Core sections**: (1) evolutionary divergence, (2) intelligence as value, (3) why agency matters

### 2) Why “Merge With the Machine” Became a Default—and Why It’s Not Inevitable
- **Slug**: `against-fatalistic-transhumanism`
- **Description**: A critique of the cultural drift toward fatalistic transhumanism. Not technophobia—just a refusal to treat biology as obsolete or surrender sovereignty to acceleration.
- **Reading time**: 7 min read
- **Tags**: Transhumanism, Ethics, Culture
- **Core sections**: (1) what transhumanism gets right, (2) where it quietly surrenders the human, (3) tools vs. telos

### 3) Biology Isn’t the Bug: Emotion, Mortality, and the Hidden Advantages of Being Alive
- **Slug**: `biology-isnt-the-bug`
- **Description**: Biology is often framed as fragile hardware. This post argues the opposite: emotion, finitude, and slow evolution are stabilizers that produce creativity, empathy, and moral discernment.
- **Reading time**: 8 min read
- **Tags**: Biology, Emotion, Consciousness
- **Core sections**: (1) what life can do that computation can’t, (2) slow evolution as wisdom, (3) why “fragility” has function

### 4) Hyperanthropism: A Life-First Alternative to Post-Human Dreams
- **Slug**: `hyperanthropism-life-first`
- **Description**: An introduction to Hyperanthropism: principled enhancement of biological intelligence, guided by sovereignty and ethics—using machines as tools, not replacing the living self.
- **Reading time**: 9 min read
- **Tags**: Hyperanthropism, Agency, Ethics
- **Core sections**: (1) definition, (2) principles of ascent, (3) what it rejects and what it preserves

### 5) Dimensional Evolution: Intelligence Doesn’t Grow Linearly
- **Slug**: `dimensional-evolution`
- **Description**: Intelligence expands across multiple axes—cognitive, emotional, moral, temporal. This post reframes “getting smarter” as layering coherence, not just adding horsepower.
- **Reading time**: 7 min read
- **Tags**: Wisdom, Systems, Consciousness
- **Core sections**: (1) layered intelligence model, (2) wholeness vs. optimization, (3) principles over trends

### 6) Sentience Must Lead: Why Calculation Can’t Replace Moral Weight
- **Slug**: `sentience-must-lead`
- **Description**: AI can simulate competence, but it can’t suffer, care, or value. This post argues that the right to steer the future belongs to sentient life—not to non-sentient computation.
- **Reading time**: 6 min read
- **Tags**: AI, Ethics, Sentience
- **Core sections**: (1) computation vs. valuation, (2) moral agency, (3) “supportive not sovereign” tools

### 7) Cognitive Nutrition: Treating the Brain Like Critical Infrastructure
- **Slug**: `cognitive-nutrition`

- **Tags**: Health, Supplements, Cognition

### 8) Emotional Intelligence Is Not Soft: It’s a High-Bandwidth Signal
- **Slug**: `emotional-intelligence-is-signal`
- **Description**: Emotion isn’t the enemy of intelligence—it’s data. This post explains why feeling is part of reasoning, and how emotional nuance becomes a compass for ethics and social reality.

### 9) The Substrate Question: Why Silicon Intelligence Isn’t Automatically “Like Us”

### 10) The Singularity as Myth: Trading Inner Work for an Outer Savior
